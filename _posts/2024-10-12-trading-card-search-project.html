A project I recently worked on with my brother. This is all based on the assumption that there are some baseball or football cards on mercari that are underpriced compared to the rest of the market. The cards title, pricing, and image would be sourced from mercari and then compared to recently sold cards on eBay. This sounded simple enough and I was fine throwing a few hundered dollars at undervalued cards in exchange for an interesting story and a potential profitable venture.

Attempt to use an already existing card search API

Initially I tried to deconstruct Ludex API so I can run the mercari images through there. The problem I ran into with Ludex was that I didn't know where the hell they are getting the card ID from. I had all of the routes, from sign in to making a card search request. All of it made sense except for the card ID which appeared once in this whole process and it was coming from the client side. I tried making a fake card ID with the same format, I even tried to re-install the app because I wanted to rule out a thought I had that they must have been sending a batch of unassigned card ID's every now & then as a security measure. You know, in case someone tries to deconstruct their API and not use it for their intended purpose. I wonder who would do that.

Anyway, the longer I spent on this search for where the mysterious Card ID came from, the crazier my ideas gotten. I decided to quit when my theory devolved into a cicadia 3301 esque idea that the card ID must have been coming from possibly a combination of one or more of the following things shoved into some type of cryptographic function: A timestamp, the image being searched, some account attribute.

Card search pivot to use eBay image search

I then briefly looked at 130point but then settled on using Ebay image API to search for already listed cards since I don't have to worry nearly as much about rate limiting or randomly getting my account cancelled for API's that aren't intended for developers (with the exception of mercari). Of course there's still a rate limit but its much more generous.

Unfortunately eBay doesn't have an image search for sold products but this was something I was okay with since I just wanted to complete this project and move on to something more important. Here is what the end product looks like

The end result

In practice we haven't found many cards that this ended up appearing as a clear buy. I don't trade cards so I was relying on my brothers opinion on the results. To me, the problem with baseball cards is that it sounds like the market is thinly traded. Football cards are better but there's only a few years that are okay to look at for them
